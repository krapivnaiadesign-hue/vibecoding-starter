Case 01 The Art of Hacking Social
THE ART * ( ) Showreel OF HACKING SOCIAL
Editorial portfolio / spatial system

The Art of Hacking Social

An experimental portfolio where the interface behaves like a composed environment: the intro mass stays present while the project field unfolds into a dense reading surface.

Role Concept, motion, UI architecture
Intent Transform cards into an editorial field
Approach

Instead of page changes, the system redistributes visual weight. The intro anchor compresses, the case expands, and the remaining works harden into a secondary stack. The geometry stays continuous.

Reading rhythm

Large headings, generous negative space and cinematic pacing turn each block into a chapter rather than a widget. The content scrolls inside the expanded card so the composition remains stable.

  • Continuous transformation instead of modals.
  • Stable typography during motion.
  • Monumental preview language preserved in detail mode.
Case 02 Liquid Trust Ledger
LIQUID TRUST LEDGER
Fintech platform / information design

Liquid Trust Ledger

A financial operations layer designed to make risk legible through staged density, soft hierarchy and compositional pressure instead of dashboard clutter.

Problem

Compliance-heavy products often flatten meaning into generic tables. This concept reframes transactional data as a narrative field where sequence, confidence and anomaly become spatial relationships.

Design system
  • Quiet monochrome base with strategic color pressure.
  • Editorial columns instead of dashboard cards.
  • Motion built from compression, drag and expansion.

The result feels less like software chrome and more like a control room for attention, where the most critical states gain physical presence without breaking the atmosphere.

Case 03 Signal Atlas for AI teams
SIGNAL ATLAS FOR AI TEAMS
AI collaboration / systems narrative

Signal Atlas

A concept for teams that need to read prompts, outputs and model decisions as living material. The interface treats collaboration as a choreography of fragments rather than a task board.

Spatial idea

Conversation clusters, model confidence and human interventions are stacked into an atlas that can tighten into summaries or unfold into essays. The same surface supports scanning and deep reading.

Outcome

By prioritizing continuity of space, the product feels more like a studio wall than a software dashboard. It invites interpretation, not just execution.

  • Composable information density.
  • Reduced cognitive snapping between states.
  • Cinematic interaction language for complex tools.
